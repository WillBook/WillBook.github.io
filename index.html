Welcome

I am currently a postdoc at University of Chicago, interested in the qualitative and quantitative behavior of solutions to differential equations, especially for models arising from fluid mechanics and kinetic theory.

My research focuses on energy methods that can be applied to obtain well-posedness and stability in nonlinear or nonlocal dynamics.

Please feel free to contact me with any questions about my work at wgolding@uchicago.edu.

Teaching

My goal as an instructor is to create a supportive and rigorous environment where students build strong foundations in mathematical reasoning and problem solving. I emphasize intuitive understanding, active learning, and the connections between theory and real-world phenomena.

Current & Recent Courses

  • Analysis I–II (Math 20200/20300), University of Chicago, 2023–24
    Advanced undergraduate analysis, including sequences, series, limits, continuity, and an introduction to measure theory.
  • Partial Differential Equations (Math 20600), University of Chicago, 2022–23
    Topics in linear and nonlinear PDEs, boundary value problems, and applications to physics.
  • Calculus I–II (Math 15100/15200), Princeton University, 2021
    Introduction to calculus concepts, techniques, and their applications for undergraduates in STEM disciplines.
